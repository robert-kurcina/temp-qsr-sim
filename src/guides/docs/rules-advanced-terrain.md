# Advanced Rules - Terrain

**Rule Level:** Advanced ()

---

## Environment & Terrain

These are the standard rules for setting up a Battlefield.

A players' first battlefield may just be a table or desk with books and cups strewn about. Or it could have lovingly crafted terrain for hills, trees, buildings, rivers. Regardless there are certain guidelines that should be followed to help keep the pace of a game quick and interesting.

---

## The Battlefield

The size of a battlefield depends on the **game size**, which is primarily depending on **model count**.

### Battlefield Sizes

| Game Size | Standard Battlefield | Larger Battlefield |
|-----------|---------------------|-------------------|
| Small | 24" × 24" | 36" × 24" |
| Medium | 36" × 36" | 48" × 36" |
| Large | 48" × 48" | 72" × 48" |

### Guidelines

- Players may always optionally use the **larger battlefields**. Deploy models along the wider battlefield edges
- If the genre is such that **ranged weapons** or **high movement rates** are common; then use the larger battlefields and have players deploy models at the **shorter battlefield edges**

---

## Terrain Elements

Battlefields comprise of a good surface area known as a "**Field**". Place enough terrain to make the battlefield interesting with enough room to maneuver.

### Layout Guidelines

- Provide numerous **side-channels** multiple paths for movement through the battlefield
- **Limit open areas** to no more **8" in length maximum** otherwise range combat will dominate the game-play
- If the genre encourages lots of range combat or has characters with high movement rates; try using a **larger battlefield** or start deployment from the **corners** of the play-area instead
- If there are **too many choke-points**, game sessions will slow down if most of the characters use range combat
- Keep the width between terrain elements at least **2"** so that models may fit between, and for tall terrain at least **4"** so that players hands may fit within them

---

## Categories of Terrain

Terrain is categorized in one or more of: **Walls**, **Obstacles**, **Areas**, or **Fields**.

| Category | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **Fields** | Relatively smooth flat terrain upon the battlefield |
| **Walls** | Terrain with large surfaces that rise vertically |
| **Obstacles** | Everything not Fields but small enough to navigate around |
| **Area** | Areas of the battlefield that have an agreed general effect, usually to act as Cover terrain, to be Rough terrain, or even both |

## Configuring Terrain

When a battlefield is configured for a Mission, players should clearly designate what sorts of **Tricky Terrain** exist for Fields, Areas, and terrain locations.

### Defaults

The defaults for Fields are as follows:

| Terrain Type | Description |
|--------------|-------------|
| **Clear** | This is the default for all fields |
| **Rough** | Rocky areas and fields, going up hill |
| **Difficult** | Rocky and going up hill |
| **Obscuring** | Crops, underbrush, elephant grass |
| **Impassable** | Walls and cliff-faces |

### Tricky Terrain

These additional terrain types provide hooks for keywords and game features such as Electronics, [Fizzle] and Fire.

| Terrain Type | Description |
|--------------|-------------|
| **Flammable*** | Grassy areas and fields |
| **Uneven*** | Cluttered areas |
| **Mire*** | Muddy areas |
| **Wet*** | Grassy areas if recently rained |
| **Water*** | Rivers, streams, ponds, and shores |

---

## Area Terrain ()

**Area Terrain** are patches of terrain placed upon the battlefield representing either **Rough**, **Difficult**, or even **Impassable** terrain. Add keywords for **Obscuring** and **Uneven**. Additional terrain elements, such as Obstacles or Walls, can always be placed atop Area terrain.

### Properties

- Area terrain should be **large enough to be significant**
- Area terrain must specify its **Movement effect**; **Clear**, **Rough**, or **Difficult**. Use **Clear** by default
- If necessary, demarcate the area of the terrain using buildings, trees, furniture, or something else

### Common Area Terrain

| Type | Effect |
|------|--------|
| **Clear** | Doesn't block LOS nor penalize movement |
| **Difficult** | May only assign as Area. Models entering or starting Difficult terrain must stop, or acquire a Delay token to continue movement. Models moving through this area must pay **2 MU for each 1 MU moved** |
| **Rough** | May only assign as Area. Models moving through this area must pay **2 MU for each 1 MU moved** |
| **Impassable** | May only assign as Area. This terrain may not be entered, moved through, or repositioned within by any model |

### Tricky Terrain Keywords

These can be paired with other Area terrain.

#### Uneven

Combine with any other Area terrain.

- **Penalizes -1 Modifier die Close Combat Hit Tests**
- The **Surefooted** trait has **no benefit** for Uneven terrain

#### Mire

**Wet**. May only assign as Area. Terrain is **Rough** if flat. It is **Difficult** if on a slope such as a hill or a ramp.

- The trait **Surefooted** does **not benefit** within Mire
- Any traits with the **Movement keyword** are **reduced a level**, and Bonus Actions involving repositioning require **+1 cascade**
- When falling into Mire terrain, **reduces misses by 1**

#### Obscuring X

Combine with any other Area terrain.

- If LOS crosses into at least **1 MU** of any Obscuring terrain, the target receives **+1 Modifier die Defender Range Combat Hit Test**
- For every **1 MU** that a model is within this terrain, the **Visibility OR to it is reduced by X MU**
- Models at **higher elevation** ignore the first **1"** to a target into Area Terrain per **1" higher** for every **1" distant**

#### Water

**Wet**. May only assign as Area.

- When an Item with the **Electronic** or **Fizzle** keyword is placed into Water terrain becomes KO'd or Eliminated on Water terrain, that Item is **disabled** for the rest of the Mission
- Use the **Disabled!** (known as "**Jammed**") token
