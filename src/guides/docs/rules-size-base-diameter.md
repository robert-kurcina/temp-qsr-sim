# Size & Base Diameter

This document anchors miniature base diameters to SIZ using mass in kilograms (kg). Humans are roughly 100 kg, which corresponds to SIZ 3. SIZ 3 is 1 MU. In physical play, bases are commonly fender washers. In metric, a 30mm washer is used; this is 1.18-inches. In the USA, the closest widely available fender washer is 1.25-inch. Therefore 1 MU is 1.25-inch. Idiomatically, this is written as "1-inch" or 1" using straight double-quotation marks.

Spiders and similar animals have splayed legs and should be mounted on washers one size larger in diameter (+1 SIZ in diameter).

## Base-contact & Base-height

When the bases of two models touch, they are in "base-contact". Bases of models should not overlap those of other models especially between models of characters which are hostile to each other ("Opposing").

Having base-diameters vary with SIZ allows for models which are on smaller bases to greatly outnumber models of larger bases when in base-contact.

A model's base-height is its base-diameter if Humanoid, and Beasts are about half the normal base-height.

When a character is placed upon the battlefield play-area, they occupy a space equal to a circle of their base-diameter as determined by their SIZ. For example, SIZ 3 occupies a circle that is 1 MU in diameter (base-diameter).

## Imprecision Allowance (0.5 MU)

Physical game-play cannot guarantee perfect placement of terrain and models. As a guideline for fair-play and sportsmanship, players may apply an imprecision allowance of about 0.5 MU **by mutual agreement**. This allowance is not intended to create advantage.

This imprecision allowance applies to:
- Determining edge-to-edge contact ("base-contact") with another model or terrain
- Movement measurements and whether a model reaches a position
- Whether a model is behind Cover for LOS checks when targeted
- Traits and rules that depend on spatial proximity, including Perimeter, Reach, Overreach, and Melee Range

This affects how the battlefield logic and spatial representation should interpret a model's position and distance checks, allowing a tolerance of up to 0.5 MU when resolving these situations.
