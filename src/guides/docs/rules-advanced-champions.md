# Advanced Rules - Champions & LoA

**Rule Level:** Advanced ()

---

## Level of Absurdity

As greater discoveries into the inner working of the universe become revealed through the understanding of the **GUTOE** or "**Grand Unified Theory of Everything**", civilization progresses technologically. Very rapidly. It consumes more matter, more energy, and expands ever more rapidly across all of known reality. What is considered reality itself, beginning with "**The Solidness**", starts expanding to include higher and more dimensions. What was once considered "**absurd**" becomes possible. What was once merely "**possible**" becomes wide-spread.

Every genre and setting have a "**Level-of-Absurdity**" [**LoA**] rating which determines which traits could be possible for assignment to characters within it.

### LoA Scale

| LoA | Description | Kardeshev Type | Era |
|-----|-------------|----------------|-----|
| 0 | Limited to what the real-world is known to have | Type 0 | Pre-2100 CE |
| 1 | Kardeshev Type I | Type I | Quantum Age, 2100 CE |
| 2 | Kardeshev Type II | Type II | Energy Age, 2250 CE |
| 3 | Kardeshev Type III | Type III | Gravity Age, 3000 CE |
| 4 | Kardeshev Type IV | Type IV | Psionic Age, 3350 CE |
| 5 | Kardeshev Type V | Type V | Symbolic Age, 5000 CE |

Each LoA defaults to **1× the Kardeshev Type** given for a Technological Age.

---

## Permeations of Absurdity

Every setting within MEST Tactics has a basic LoA which permeates the setting. This is usually the result of innate progresses in technology becoming prevalent. Sometimes it is the result of "**leakages**" between closely parallel universes or "**Frame Parallels**".

### Innate

Genres with pulp, fantastic, or super-science elements will usually have an LoA matching the element requiring the highest Kardeshev Type.

### Leakage

Genres before Kardeshev Type I that have anachronistic elements, because of leakages, will have a higher LoA.

---

## Determining Leakage

When a futuristic element is introduced into a setting, adjust the LoA by **1-2-3-4** if it is too early by **1-2-5-10 Ages**.

**Examples:**
- Allowing the **Medieval Age Longbow** weapon within the **Dark Ages** is **LoA 1**
- Allowing **Mechanized Warfare ("tanks")** from the **Modern Age** into the **Industrial Age** is **LoA 2**

Alternatively, leakages from higher Kardeshev types (which have higher LoA) can always appear in Ages with a lower type. Merely use the higher LoA.

**Example:** Magic and Arcanics is Kardeshev Type IV, and therefore LoA 4. Having Magic appear in the Stone Age, or the Medieval Age, or the Quantum Age is all the same. These become **LoA 4**.

---

## Synonyms

A **synonym** is when an Item which isn't normally available is renamed. Usually it is a later Technological Age or if it is a different Item class (Weapon, Armor, Equipment), or of a different Item type (e.g.; Helmet, Shield, Gear, Suit).

- **Increase by +1 LoA for each**
- **Set LoA to 5 or less**

**Example:** Assigning the capabilities of a **Shotgun** (Modern Age ranged weapon) to a **Dagger** (Stone Age melee weapon) is **LoA 3 + 2 LoA = 5**, which is the maximum.

## Thinning the Codon Barrier

The **Codon Barrier** is thinner when the setting has a higher Level-of-Absurdity. This creates a few effects for games set within MEST Tactics.

### Backdrop

The first is that it allows crazier technologies to be introduced into a setting. This is mostly a narrative concern to make for a cool story. It also allows Missions to be completed with unusual archetypes, weapons, armor, and equipment.

### Champions

Champions warp reality about them because they are attempting to escape across the Codon Barrier. When an area has a high amount of Absurdity, this makes it easier for them to escape.

### Distortion

"**Reality Distortion Field**". Champions alter reality around them. As they progress through Campaigns, they'll acquire the **Reputation** trait which allows, possibly, higher LoA for their Assembly at any Mission when they are deployed.

## Mechanism

As a result of the Codon Barrier, not every Frame Parallel allows magic or super-science to exist even though a Champion has embodied a host character.

### Basic LoA

A character has a basic LoA matching the traits it has been assigned which has the **highest LoA**.

- Most traits will have a **default LoA of zero**. This includes such things which are reasonably possible for a human being ("Human") to have such as the **Fight** or **Sneaky**
- **LoA of 1** usually are assigned to Animals and Humanoids
- Some traits will have a **higher LoA** to represent the fact that Humans as we know them wouldn't necessarily have them except as elements of a pulp, cinematic, punk, or fantasy genres. Those are in increasing LoA order

This ensures that the trait **Intense** is **LoA 1**, while the trait **Precise** is **LoA 2**. And it follows that the **Sorcerer** trait is **LoA 3**.

### Champion Alteration

When a host is about to be altered by the Champion pattern, determine if the additional levels of a trait could possibly exist for the genre's LoA.

- **Reduce the effective LoA by 1** if the trait would be applied to an Animal or to a non-Human
- **Reduce the effect LoA by the Reputation level** of the Champion

If it could not exist, **reduce the trait levels to be added**.

## Higher Skill Levels

Characters may acquire Skills at higher than level 3. This is possible but inefficient.

### Cost

A trait may be acquired at **level 4, 5, or 6**. This will cost:
- **Level 4:** 10× the Time Unit and the specified amount of XP
- **Level 5:** 20× the Time Unit and the specified amount of XP
- **Level 6:** 50× the Time Unit and the specified amount of XP

### LoA Impact

Acquiring a trait at level 4 or more will affect a character's **Level-of-Absurdity [LoA]**:
- At **level 4**, the character's effective LoA is **increased by 1**
- At **level 6**, the LoA is **increased by 2**

If the effective LoA is higher than allowed by the genre, **disallow increasing the Skill to that level**.

---

## Attribute Upgrades and LoA

Champions with Attribute Upgrades might also select a Baseline host which already has its own Attribute Upgrades. This may affect the Level-of-Absurdity.

- Each **+++** instead becomes a **+1 Attribute value**
- If the total of any Attribute improvement is **+2 or more**, the LoA is **increased by 1** for that character
- If **3 Attributes** are at this, the LoA is **increased by 2**
- If **6 or more Attributes** are at this, the LoA is **increased by 3**

---

## Altering Intellect

The **[Beast!]**, **[Beast]**, **[Beast+]**, and **[Primitive]** traits are sequentially tied together as aspects of Intellect which is a higher-order pattern for Champions.

### Intellect Traits Removal Table

| Trait | XP Cost | LoA | Notes |
|-------|---------|-----|-------|
| [Beast!] | 11 | 2 | As [Beast] |
| [Beast] | 8 | 1 | As [Beast+] |
| [Beast+] | 6 | 1 | As [Primitive] |
| [Primitive] | 2 | 0 | - |

As a result, these Disabilities associated with Intellect are very hard to remove as each only slightly improves how much control a Champion has over the next such host.

- For **11 XP** → **[Beast!]** becomes **[Beast]**
- For **8 XP** → **[Beast]** becomes **[Beast+]**
- For **6 XP** → **[Beast+]** becomes **[Primitive]**
- For **2 XP** → **[Primitive]** is removed

The **[Beast!]** trait has an **LoA of 2** to remove. Assign any character which had this trait **+2 LoA** if it becomes **[Beast+]** or removes **[Beast!]** entirely.

The **[Beast+]** trait has an **LoA of 1** to remove. Assign any character which had this trait **+1 LoA** if it removes this trait entirely.

## Escalation of Intent

A Player's set of Champions exists upon the Roster for a relatively short time. Each Champion, being embodiments themselves of higher-order Thought Beings, yearns to grow to be free of the prison that is the Codon Barrier to rejoin the **Dios Primin**, the **First Thought**, within the entirety of the **Ovodium Cosmogos**.

### Reputation

All Champions will eventually and automatically acquire the **Reputation** trait.

- Each Champion which has acquired **at least 1 XP in each of every 6 Missions** participated, receives **+1 Reputation**
- **Reputation** behaves like the **Fear** trait, but it is something which all living creatures, especially intelligent ones, can recognize

### Reputation X ()

**Intrinsic. Psychology.**

For every Reputation level higher than the target within **Visibility and LOS**, receive **+1 Modifier die for all Opposed tests** against that target.

### Reality Distortion Field

If that target could be affected adversely by several Opposing characters with Reputation, use **only the highest difference** in Reputation levels.

- If the target has one or more of these **Outsider**, **Invader**, **Mythos**, **Fear**, or **Beast** traits, then Reputation will **not** affect them unless also have those same traits
- If Opposing models have the **Reputation** trait, **reduce X by the lower rating** experienced by either model

### Embodiment Limit

A character is allowed at most **1 Virtuosity trait per Reputation level**, and a maximum of **Reputation X trait levels** for any Virtuosity trait.

### LoA Limit

Allowed LoA is increased to the **highest Reputation level** of any Friendly character on the battlefield.

## Reality Distortion Fields

A key game-feature of Champions is that they create localized "**reality distortion fields**" around them.

When they are deployed with an Assembly, it allows archetypes, weapons, armor, and equipment to be used from settings with a **Level-of-Absurdity [LoA]** to match that Champion's **Reputation** trait level.

### Restrictions

- Do **not** assign archetypes to an Assembly if it requires a higher LoA than the setting
- Do **not** assign Packages or Upgrades to Baselines or Champions if these require a higher LoA than the setting
- Do **not** assign Weapons, Armor, or Equipment from higher LoA lists to any characters in a setting with a lower LoA
- Do **not** assign Fantastic Items keywords which require higher LoA to any Items in a setting with a lower LoA

### Exception

The exception is if the Assembly has a **Champion with a Reputation trait level at or higher than the required LoA**.

## The Intent of Champions

Each player's Champions will begin to learn enough from its period of confinement within the Frame Parallels and eventually understand how to escape the Codon Barrier.

### Champion's Intent Test

At the end of a Mission, as soon as a Champion receives the **Reputation trait**:

1. Its Player must perform a **Champion's Intent Test** as a **+X Modifier die Unopposed Reputation Test**
2. For **X**, use the **LoA of the setting** where the Mission took place. The higher the LoA, the easier the Test
3. Upon **passing**, the Champion has learned enough about reality to **escape the Codon Barrier**. It is **removed from the Roster**

### Opportunity

For each **Friendly Champion** which has escaped for this Mission, receive **+1 Base die** when checking other Friendly Champions which were also deployed.

### Campaign Victory Points

The very first Champion of each Side which has escaped for any Mission awards its Side **+1 CVP**.
