# Missions & Game Structure

This module outlines the framework for playing a game of MEST Tactics, from setting up the battlefield to determining a winner. A game is referred to as a Mission.

## 1. Mission Setup

Before a Mission begins, players must set up the battlefield and deploy their forces.

1.  **Define Battlefield:** Players agree on the dimensions of the playing area (e.g., 3'x3' or 4'x4').
2.  **Place Terrain:** Players take turns placing terrain features onto the battlefield. Terrain should be placed to create an interesting and balanced environment with a mix of open ground, cover, and obstacles.
3.  **Determine Deployment Zones:** Each player is assigned a deployment zone, typically the area within 6" of one of the battlefield edges.
4.  **Deploy Forces:** Players take turns placing their character models within their designated deployment zone. Characters must be placed more than 1" away from any Opposing models.

## 2. Game Rounds and Turns

A Mission is played over a series of Game Rounds. Each Round consists of a series of Turns.

1.  **Initiative Phase:** At the start of each Game Round, both players make an Opposed INT Test. This is known as the Initiative Roll. The player who wins the roll gains the Initiative for that round.
2.  **Activation Phase:** The player with the Initiative chooses one of their Ready characters and Activates it. After that character's activation is complete, the other player chooses one of their Ready characters to Activate.
3.  **Turns:** Players continue to alternate activating characters until all characters on the battlefield have a Done token.
4.  **End Phase:** Once all characters are Done, the Game Round ends. Remove all Done tokens from characters, making them Ready for the next round. Check for any end-of-round effects.

The Mission continues with new Game Rounds until an End-Game Trigger is met.

## 3. Mission Objectives & Victory Conditions

Victory is determined by achieving Mission Objectives and accumulating Victory Points (VP). Unless a specific scenario states otherwise, the default Mission is **Elimination**.

### Elimination

The primary objective is to neutralize the opponent's forces.

*   **Scoring Victory Points:**
    *   **KO an Opponent:** You score 1 VP for each Opposing character that is KO'd.
    *   **Eliminate an Opponent:** You score 2 VP for each Opposing character that is Eliminated.
*   **Character Bounties:** Some characters may be worth additional VP as determined by the scenario or by their BP cost (e.g., 1 additional VP for every 50 BP).

## 4. End-Game Triggers

The Mission ends when one of the following conditions is met:

*   **Last Man Standing:** The game ends immediately if a player has no more In-Play characters on the battlefield.
*   **Round Limit:** The game ends at the conclusion of a pre-determined number of Game Rounds (e.g., 5 Rounds).
*   **Sudden Death:** If both players have an equal number of VP at the end of the final Game Round, the player who currently holds the Initiative wins.

At the end of the game, the player with the most Victory Points is declared the winner.
