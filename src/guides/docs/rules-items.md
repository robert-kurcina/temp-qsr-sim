# Items

Players may spend BP to purchase and assign Items (“Items”) to their characters, which are weapons, armors, and equipment.

*   Assign Items reasonably to characters according to how they are sculpted.
*   All characters, weapons, armor, and equipment are rated with a BP cost.

For example; a Common Average character costs 30 BP and a Medium Revolver is 33 BP. Together this character when armed with the weapon is 63 BP.

For example; a Common Untrained character costs 7 BP and Daggers cost 6 BP. Together this costs 13 BP.

*   Characters and Items are also given traits which are little rules that alter or break the rules in the game. Most traits, the Ability traits, are beneficial, but Disability traits have penalty effects. See the Traits List section for more information.

## Available Hands

A character is allowed a number of hands (“Hand”) equal to its model's sculpt, including prehensile tails and trunks, tentacles, or other. All Humanoid characters are presumed to have two Hands and can normally wield one Item each.

*   Items require a commitment of 1 or 2 hands, noted as [1H] and [2H]. These then become “In Hand”.
*   All Fiddle actions must commit 1 available Hand each.
*   A character may commit with one less available Hand, but this causes a penalty of -1 Base die for any Fiddle Tests, and for the very next Test performed when interrupted by a React.

See the list of Traits regarding the [1H] and [2H] traits for more information on how this affects game-play.

**A Common Example:**

The most common example is outfitting a character with a Shield [1H] and a Spear [2H].

This would require three Hands, but most humanoid characters have just two. This character can use the Spear with one fewer Hand. However, if it is interrupted and attacked during a React it will suffer a -1 Base die penalty.

**Another Common Example:**

A character could be outfitted with multiple items requiring Hands. Such as having Longbow and a Dagger, or having two Spears. A player may decide that the additional items are stowed somewhere on their person. They may use the Fiddle action for each item or pair to switch out.

## Weapons

The general rule for assigning weapons to a character is to examine the sculpt of the associated model; if it has a weapon, then it qualifies to have a weapon assigned to it.

*   Each weapon type may have a variety of choices available; a player could decide that a model's over-large knife is a Dagger, a Saber, or maybe a Sword.
*   Characters pay full BP value for the highest cost Range Combat (Bow, Firearm, Range, Support, Thrown, Throwable) and Close Combat (Daggers, Clubs, Swords, etc.) weapons; other weapons are half-cost. Round up to nearest BP.
    *   Some models may have two or more weapons sculpted onto them; a player can choose to pay for just those intended for use. A weapon not paid-for can't be used.
    *   Models with no weapons in their sculpt may be created as having either Improvised or Unarmed, with Improvised as the default which costs zero BP.
    *   Weapons with the Conceal or Discrete traits allow one purchase regardless of if the model has the proper sculpt.

### Reading the Weapons List:

Any entries which have only a dash (-) are considered to either not be relevant (such as for OR) or be empty (such as for Traits). Each entry in the various Weapon lists is described with these properties:

*   **Name** — The name of the weapon. Some weapons have name modifiers such as “Small”, “Large”, or “Medium”. Weapons which have “Medium” as part of their name are the default and can be written without that modifier. Therefore, “Spear” and “Spear, Medium” are identical.
*   **Class** — The class of the weapon; usually either Thrown, Bow, or Melee. Also, Firearm, Natural, Range, and Support.
*   **OR** — Optimal Range. Used to attack targets at range. Melee weapons usually don’t have an OR entry unless they also have the Throwable trait, show as either ‘-‘ or N/A.
*   **Acc** — Accuracy. Used for Attacker Hit Tests. If listed in square brackets, the penalty shown is for Attacker Close Combat Hit Tests, and for Defender Close Combat Hit Tests.
*   **Impact** — Impact rating or “I”. Used for Attacker Damage Tests. Each Impact rating reduces 1 total Armor rating [ AR ] from the target if it wears Armor for Attacker Damage Test.
*   **Damage** — The Damage Rating used for the Attacker Damage Tests. This can be a “flat value” (such as STR or just ‘5’) and may also include dice (the “dice value” such as +2m or -1b). If empty, there is no Damage Test unless by traits.
*   **STR** — Items identified with STR for OR or Damage use a flat value equal to the character's STR attribute.
*   **Traits** — One or more traits may be listed and separated by periods. Disability Traits are listed in square brackets.
*   **BP** — Build Points to purchase this Item.

### Unarmed or Improvised

A character is Unarmed by default unless it has at least one other weapon which it can use in Close Combat. Improvised weapons represent the use of some tool, rock, stick, or element of terrain.

*   A weapon such as a Bow or Rifle can’t be normally used in Close Combat and becomes an Improvised Melee weapon automatically for that purpose.
*   A Sword or Helmet, which can’t normally be used in Range Combat, becomes an Improvised Range weapon automatically when thrown and used for that purpose.

## Armor

What can be considered armor varies greatly from genre to genre. The majority of genres have some equivalent of the Helm, and Gear types of armor.

*   A character is allowed just one of each type of armor such as Helm, Gear, Shield, or Suit.
    *   **Helm** — Helms cover the head of a character and must be purchased separate from any Suit.
    *   **Gear** — Represent small bits of armor and padding covering arms, shoulders, shins, or abdomen.
    *   **Shield** — Held in hand to help block and deflect.
*   For WYSIWYG consistency, “Suits” cover the body as;
    *   **Light** — Light Armor is usually when just the front of the torso of a sculpt is covered, metal or otherwise. Or has a full torso covered in hide. Weighs about 5 kg.
    *   **Medium** — Medium Armor has the front and back of the torso covered with hide or metal, and maybe the legs or arms though not entirely. Weighs about 10 kg.
    *   **Heavy** — Heavy Armor is segmented and the entire body excluding the head and hands are covered in very thick hide or all metal with some padding. Weighs about 20 kg.

### Reading the Armor List:

Any entries which have only a dash (-) are considered to either not be relevant (such as for AR), or be empty (such as for Traits). Each entry in the various Armor lists is described with these properties:

*   **Name** — The name of the armor. Some armors have name modifiers such as “Small”, “Large”, or “Medium”. Armors which have “Medium” as part of their name are the default and can be written without that modifier. Therefore, “Shield” and “Shield, Medium” are identical.
*   **Type** — The type of armor. Limits usage per the above rules.
*   **AR** — Armor rating [ AR ] via the Armor trait. Used for Defender Damage Tests.

Each AR reduces the Wound damage that a character receives from an attack by one. An Attacker’s weapon will have an Impact [ I ] rating which will normally reduce total AR by 1 each. Whenever an Attacker uses the Concentrate action, it reduces each Item that a target has by 3 AR.

See the In Consideration of Armor section for more information.

*   **Traits** — One or more traits may be listed and separated by periods. Disability Traits are listed in square brackets.
*   **BP** — Build Points to purchase this Item.

### Reading the Equipment List:

Any entries which have only a dash (-) are considered to either not be relevant or be empty (such as for Traits). Each entry in the various Equipment lists is described with these properties:

*   **Name** — The name of the equipment.
*   **Class** — The class of the equipment; Accessory, Attachment, Gear, Helm, Medicine, Munitions, Suit, Tool, or Upgrade. If Helm, Gear, or Suit is identified; these count as being armor of that type.
*   **Traits** — One or more traits may be listed and separated by periods. Disability Traits are listed in square brackets.
*   **BP** — Build Points to purchase this Item.
