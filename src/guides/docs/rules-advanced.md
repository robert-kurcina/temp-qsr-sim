# Advanced Rules

This module introduces optional rules that add tactical depth and complexity to the game. These rules alter the normal flow of play and are recommended for experienced players.

## 1. Passive Player Actions & Choices

Passive players are not just idle observers. When their characters are targeted, they have choices they can make to improve their odds of survival.

### Defend!

When a character is declared as the target of a Close Combat or Range Combat Attack, its controlling player may declare that the character will **Defend!** This choice is made before the Attacker rolls their Hit Test.

*   **Effect:** The defending character receives a **+1b (Bonus Base Die)** on their Defender Hit Test against the incoming attack. This bonus is listed in the Situational Test Modifiers.
*   **Cost:** After the attack is fully resolved, the defending character immediately acquires a **Delay token**. This represents their focus on pure defense at the expense of their readiness to act.

## 2. Reacts ()

Reactions, or “Reacts,” are special actions that allow a player to interrupt an Opposing character's action. The character performing the React temporarily becomes the Active character.

### How to Perform a React

A character can perform a React in one of two ways:

1.  **From Wait Status:** A character that has performed the **Wait** action can expend their Wait status to perform an **Overwatch** React.
2.  **Via a Trait:** Some traits, like **Counter-strike!**, grant the ability to perform a specific React when a certain condition is met. These Reacts often cost AP.

### General React Rules

*   A character may only perform one React per trigger.
*   If multiple characters can React to the same trigger, the player whose turn is being interrupted decides the order in which the Reacts are resolved.
*   A React is fully resolved before the action that triggered it continues. If the React causes the original Active character to be KO’d or acquire a Delay token, their action is immediately cancelled and any AP spent are lost. The action has been **Countered**.

### Types of Reacts

#### Overwatch (from Wait Status)

This is the primary React granted by the **Wait** action. It allows a character to control a lane of fire and punish enemy movement.

*   **Trigger:** An Opposing character moves at least half its base-diameter while within the Reacting character's Line of Sight (LOS).
*   **Action:** The character in Wait status may perform a **Range Combat Attack** against the moving character. This React can be declared at any point during the opponent's movement.
*   **Effect:** If the Overwatch attack is successful and causes a Wound or a Stun effect, the target's action ends immediately. They may not complete their move.

#### Opportunity Attack

Characters engaged in melee are a constant threat, even when it isn't their turn.

*   **Trigger:** An Opposing character that is Engaged with the Reacting character attempts to perform any action other than a Close Combat Attack, Disengage, or Fiddle action against the Reacting character.
*   **Action:** The Reacting character may immediately perform a **Close Combat Attack** against the Opposing character.
*   **Cost:** This React costs 1 AP.

#### Counter-strike! (Trait-based)

This powerful React is granted by the **Counter-strike!** trait.

*   **Trigger:** An Opponent makes a Close Combat Attack against the character and misses (the Attacker fails their Hit Test).
*   **Action:** The character with the trait may perform a **Close Combat Attack** back at the original Attacker.
*   **Cost:** This React costs 1 AP, as specified by the trait.
