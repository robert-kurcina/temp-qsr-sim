# Advanced Rules - Fire

**Rule Level:** Advanced ()

Fire is a dynamic terrain feature generated by certain traits, spells, weapons, or Items of equipment.

---

## Fire Markers

Missions may specify that Fire markers are deployed upon the battlefield. Some weapons also deploy Fire markers.

### Size

Fire markers are given a size in "**Fire points**" which correspond to marker dimensions:

| Fire Points | Diameter | Height |
|-------------|----------|--------|
| 1 | 0.75 MU | ~1.5 MU |
| 2 | 1 MU | ~2 MU |
| 3 | 2 MU | ~4 MU |
| 4 | 3 MU | ~6 MU |

Height is about twice the base-diameter to represent smoke.

### Properties

- Fire markers generate **Obscuring terrain** within 1" up to **twice its base-diameter in height**
- Models may **cross** but **not stand upon** Fire markers
- Fire markers behave as **Soft Cover**
- Fire markers have **Light X**, where X is its **Fire points + 6**

### Additional Light

Additional Light X is generated in any given **4 MU diameter area**:
- Plus 1 for a second of equal X, or
- Four at X minus 1

---

## Fire Effects

At the start of a character's Initiative, determine how many Fire markers are in **base-contact**.

### Scorch Test

Perform a "**Scorch Test**" as an **Unopposed REF Test** at **-1 Modifier die per Fire marker** in base-contact.

### Burned Token Assignment

Upon fail, the model is subject to an **Unopposed SIZ Test** as a Damage Test; adjusted by **-1 Modifier die for each of those misses**.

Upon failing the Damage Test, it is assigned a **Burned token for every miss**.

---

## Spreading Fire

Fire markers in base-contact with **Flammable terrain** may "**Spread Fire**".

### Spread Fire Check

Check "Spread Fire" at the **end of each Turn** for every Fire marker by rolling **two Modifier dice**.

- Roll an **additional Modifier die** if the Fire marker is already **size 4 (3 MU)** in diameter. Fire markers will spread faster upwards
- Roll an **additional Modifier die** if spreading upwards

### Results

- Upon **success**, **increase the size** of the Fire marker
- For each **additional success**, place a **size 1 Fire marker** in base-contact to the current Fire marker at the Flammable terrain. If necessary, use the Unbiased Scatter diagram
- If the "Spread Fire" check results in **3 successes**, a **Gas:Blacksmoke Puff marker** is also generated and placed in base-contact

---

## Reducing Fire Markers

Fires very soon disappear by being "**reduced**".

### End of Turn Check

At the end of the current Player's Turn:

1. Check every Fire marker which is **not on Flammable terrain** (this is the default)
2. Roll **two Modifier dice**
3. If the terrain is **indoors** such as linoleum, marble, concrete or cement, sand, rocks, dirt, etc.: **automatically reduce each Fire marker by one** before rolling the dice
4. If there are **no successes**, **reduce its size by 1**
5. **Remove** the Fire marker if it is **less than size 1**

### Wet Terrain

**Wet terrain** is Terrain that stops Fire markers:
- All water, rivers, ponds, or areas identified with snow
- Fire markers will **never cross into Wet terrain**
- Fire markers upon Wet terrain are **reduced by two sizes** before rolling the dice
- Those which are reduced are replaced with a **Gas:Steam marker** of equal size

## Gas and Fire

Newly placed Fire markers will affect Gas markers already upon the battlefield.

### Gas Movement

- Gas markers will **never move towards** Fire markers
- All Gas markers already upon the battlefield will move, if within their own **base-diameter of a Fire marker** when it is placed, will **reposition away** the base-diameter of the Fire marker placed

### Movement Order

1. Start with the **largest Gas markers first**
2. Then all of the next size smaller
3. Repeat this until all have been repositioned

### Direction

- If the Attacker and the target location are known, reposition the Gas markers **away from the LOF** and the Attacker and target
- Otherwise use the **Unbiased Scatter diagram**
- Gas markers which become in base-contact other Gas markers reposition those others in the **same direction** and remain in base-contact

### Gas Reduction

Newly placed Gas markers which become in base-contact with Fire markers will **reduce in size** according to the size of the largest Fire marker.

- If the resulting size is **smaller than 1**, **remove the Gas marker**; it does **not Linger**

---

## Terrain Conversion

### Flammable Terrain

If a Terrain element such as a **bush**, or **tree** is affected by a Fire marker:
- **Replace** that element when placing the Fire marker

### Buildings

If a portion of a **Building** is affected by a **size 4 (3 MU) Fire marker**:
- Instead of placing additional Fire markers, **remove that portion of the Building** if possible
