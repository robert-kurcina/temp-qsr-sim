# Traits

Traits are assigned to characters, weapons, armor, and equipment. Each trait is a set of one or more small rules that enhances the main set of rules or alter the effects of another trait. Near the back of this rules book is a Traits List section showing for how each modifies the game rules.

## Overview

*   Something with a given trait can be referenced with that trait. So, a weapon with the [Flint] trait is a Flint weapon. If a trait states that there is a "target", this may refer to other models or to locations. Most traits are abilities that provide beneficial effects.
*   Some traits are disabilities which have penalty effects. Disabilities are identified within square brackets such as [Blinders], [Stub], or [Coward]. Related disabilities are written within the same bracket [Stub, Awkward].
*   Some trait entries are given an X such as Armor X or [Laden X]. These allow traits to be acquired at a certain level; the default level for such traits is always 1.
*   Some traits have variations such as [Discard+] or [Discard!]. These are [Discard] but with extra clauses.
*   Traits may have classifier keywords such as "Movement", "AoE", "Skill", "Intrinsic", "Asset", "Attack Effect", "Psychology", "Natural Weapon", or "Magic" which are hooks for other game rules.
*   Traits may have a "see" trait reference to another trait such as "Throwable — Asset. see [Discard]". The referenced trait may also apply whenever using the main trait depending on the context of that trait.
*   If a reference has an X in it such as "Stealthy X — see Night-vision X" it means that the referenced trait will be at the same level as the main trait.
*   Some traits have an arrow (">") which indicates that it is affected by whatever item or traits exist at the right of the arrow, such as Claws > Poison, or Fume > Smoke.
    *   Often what is on the right of the arrow is a dependency. Claws > Poison indicates that the Poison trait is active only when the Claws are used.
    *   Sometimes what is on the right of the arrow is a list delimited by periods and written within curly-braces such as when using weapons with the ROF trait: ROF > {[Jitter, Feed, Jam]. Burst}

## Adding Trait Levels

Whenever a trait with levels is acquired more than once to a given thing, be it a character, weapon, armor or equipment; the trait levels are added together for its default usage of that thing.

For example; a character with the Medium Armor has

AR 4 [ “Armor Rating 4” ] which when combined with a Medium Shield that has AR 1 and so the total is AR 5.

## Reducing Trait Levels

Whenever a trait mentions “reduce”, it will decrease the levels of something such as dice or a named trait by a certain amount for a very specific context. If this reduces that thing to zero or less, then regard that as not having any in-game effect for the duration of that context.

## Applying Traits

Traits normally affect just that character, weapon, armor, or equipment to which they’ve been assigned.

*   Ability traits apply whenever desired by the player controlling the model, weapon, armor, or equipment.
*   Disability traits are applied according to their descriptions. All players should take note to always apply Disability traits as often as necessary.
*   Traits always over-ride non-Trait rules but most often augment each other. Conflicting traits should be decided in favor of the Active character. Or roll a die to decide.
