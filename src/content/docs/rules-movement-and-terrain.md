# Movement and Terrain

## Measuring

One Measured Unit [ MU ] is equal to the base-diameter of a SIZ 3 model; the standard human-sized character.

This is presumed to be a 30MM [ or 1.25-inch ] circular base for 1:64 to 1:48 scale [ 25MM to 33MM ] models. MU thus changes according to scale. Models on longer bases have a base-diameter equal to that longer length.

An MU is also written as straight double-quotes and the notations are interchangeable. Therefore 1" and  2" being 1 MU and 2 MU respectively. MU (pronounced “em you”) is used interchangeably with “units”, “segments”, “spaces”, “sections”, but also “inches”.

### Overview

*   Acquire several 8" measuring sticks. These by definition are 8 MU each.
    *   Mark the stick with 1 MU segments. Also mark it with a 0.5-MU segment.
    *   Players may use one ruler-length to measure angle and distance at any time during play from their own models to elsewhere.
*   Measuring from a model is from its base up to its height. This forms a cylindrical volume for which all Line-of-Sight [ LOS ] is to be traced towards or from. Its “visible area”.
    *   Models sculpted or mounted in unusual positions, or raised bases, must be treated as though of normal height which for a SIZ 3 model is equal to its base-diameter.
    *   Long, flat, sprawling, overly tall, or spindly models can be regarded as tall as its tallest part. Have all players agree.
*   **Imprecision** — All measuring fractions round down to nearest 0.5". Positional adjustments up to 0.5" is allowed, favoring the Active model, if players agree. Games resolve faster this way.

### Game Scale

Game scale is 2 meters or yards per 1", and about 5 seconds per Turn.

*   Game-turns with very little movement or activity can be considered upwards of 30 seconds long.
*   A standard battlefield of 48" × 48" or nearly 100-meters by 100-meters. About the size of a small city-block.

## Visibility and Distance

*   **MU** — A Measured Unit. It is 1" , also 1 MU, and is equal to the base-diameter of a SIZ 3 model; the stand human-sized character.
*   **LOS** — Line-of-Sight [ LOS ] occurs if the line drawn between any point within the volume of the Active model and to within the volume of the target is not obstructed by Blocking terrain like walls, or even other models.
*   **LOF** — Line-of-Fire [ LOF ] is the line between two models and beyond the target but not necessarily in LOS.
*   **OR** — Optimal Range [ OR ] is a distance such as 6". If there are many ORs in effect, always use the smaller (the worst) one.
*   **ORM** — An OR Multiple [ ORM ] is some multiple of the OR distance towards a target model or location. Each full multiple of an OR is a ˗1 Modifier die for Detect Tests and Range Attack Hit Tests.
*   **Max ORM** — The maximum OR multiple is 3; no Test involving OR may exceed that under normal conditions.
*   **Point-blank** — Targets within half of any OR are “Point-blank”. This provides +1 Modifier die Range Combat Hit Test for attackers not Engaged to them.
*   **Lighting** — Lighting is either “Day, Clear” weather, or “Twilight, Overcast”.
*   **Visibility**  — Visibility limits all other OR and limits all range values. Use Visibility OR 16" for “Day, Clear” and OR 8" is used for “Twilight, Overcast”.
*   **Cohesion** — Models are considered to be within Cohesion if they are range and LOS of another model. The range is at best equal to half Visibility rounded down, but no more than the higher of either 4 MU or the SIZ in MUs of either model.

 Visibility may be altered by the use of Traits. Visibility may never be reduced by these adjustments to below 0.5 MU but has no upper limit.

 Ranged Combat attacks will usually have two ORs; one for the Visibility and one for the weapon or method of attack. Use the smaller OR for determining any ORM penalties.

For example; a Rifle has OR 12. If the Visibility is OR 8 and a target is at 24 MU then the ORM is 2 for the Rifle and 3 for Visibility. Using the lowest OR of 8 makes the ORM 3.

## Environment & Terrain

These are the standard rules for setting up a Battlefield.

A players’ first battlefield may just be a table or desk with books and cups strewn about. Or it could have lovingly crafted terrain for hills, trees, buildings, rivers. Regardless there are certain guidelines that should be followed to help keep the pace of a game quick and interesting.

### The Battlefield

For these QSR rules, the battlefield will always be Small and measure 24 x 24 MU.

### Terrain Elements

Battlefields comprise of a good surface area known as a “Field”. Place enough terrain to make the battlefield interesting with enough room to maneuver.

*   Provide numerous side-channels multiple paths for movement through the battlefield.
*   Limit open areas to no more 8" in length maximum otherwise range combat will dominate the game-play.
*   If the genre encourages lots of range combat or has characters with high movement rates; try using a larger battlefield or start deployment from the corners of the play-area instead.
*   If there are too many choke-points, game sessions will slow down if most of the characters use range combat.
*   Keep the width between terrain elements least 2" so that models may fit between, and for tall terrain at least 4" so that players hands may fit within them.

### Categories of Terrain

Terrain is categorized in one or more of; Walls, Obstacles, Areas, or Fields.

*   **Fields** — Relatively smooth flat terrain upon the battlefield.
*   **Walls** — Terrain with large surfaces that rise vertically.
*   **Obstacles** — Everything not Fields but small enough to navigate around.
*   **Area** — Areas of the battlefield that have an agreed general effect, usually to act as Cover terrain, to be Rough terrain, or even both.

## Configuring Terrain

When a battlefield is configured for a Mission, players should clearly designate what sorts of Tricky Terrain exist for Fields, Areas, and terrain locations.

### Defaults

The defaults for Fields are as follows:

*   **Clear** — This is the default for all fields
*   **Rough** — Rocky areas and fields, going up hill
*   **Difficult** — Rocky and going up hill
*   **Impassable** — Walls and cliff-faces

### Buildings

Terrain may also include any number of buildings.

*   Buildings are large walled, usually enclosed areas of the battlefield normally decorated with windows, doors, and maybe a roof.
*   These can be shacks, trailer homes, row houses, mansions, warehouses, gas stations, granaries, or even a laboratory, or a diner. As the genre changes, so does the kinds of buildings which appear upon the battlefield.
*   Buildings can be represented by large 3D game elements, 2.5D layouts, or even just a floor-plan map.
*   Buildings normally are used as decorative terrain, but players may agree to allow some to be entered. Agree on how the interior of these areas are represented.

#### Navigating Buildings

For these QSR rules, presume that buildings are merely giants blocks of terrain which can’t be entered.
