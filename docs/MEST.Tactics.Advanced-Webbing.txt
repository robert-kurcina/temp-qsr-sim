Webbing & Strands ()
Giant spiders and mystic chains are relatively common
features in fantasy genres. These Webbing rules work in
conjunction with the Webcaster and Webcrawler traits.
Strand Markers
Strands are created by characters with the Webcaster X
trait. Strands are represented by Strand markers.
 A Strand marker is about X × 2 MU long by 0.1 MU wide.
 These are best represented by strands of cotton fiber
twisted into the proper lengths. A 2 MU width is
 A less messy alternative are white pipe-cleaners or strings
of white yarn cut to size.
 Strand markers can swivel, warp, twist; whichever is
ideal to affect as many Opposing models as necessary
when first placed after an Attack action.
 Place Strand markers from the perspective of the
Attacking model towards the first and nearest of the
Opposing models or terrain elements.
 Warp or bend the markers as desired but maintain
contact with that first model and any which may follow.
 Morass — Strand markers are Difficult terrain.
 However, models which start in base-contact with a
Strand marker are considered as crossing Rough terrain
for the first AP spent for Movement.
 Each Strand marker in base-contact with a target causes a
penalty of -1 MOV, and are also penalized -1 Wild die
Defender Combat Tests.

Removing Strands
 Models may remove Strand markers from base-contact,
or assigned to themselves or to any model in base-
contact by performing a Fiddle action using an
Unopposed Physicality (higher of STR or SIZ) Test.
 Upon passing remove one Strand from base-contact and
move it out of base-contact up to cascades × 1" away.
 For every 3 cascades, do this for an additional Strand
marker.
 At the end of the Turn, roll 1 Modifier dice for each
group of Strands within 1-inch of each other. For each
success, remove 1 Strand marker from the group.
 Strand markers in base-contact with Fire markers, or has
the Fire trait, are immediately removed.
